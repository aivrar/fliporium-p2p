What it is

Fliporium is a small desktop hangout for you and the few people you actually care about — no signup, and no company sitting in the middle. It's one portable .exe: keep it on a USB stick if you like, and your identity and chat history travel with the file. Create a room, share its invite link, and whoever has it connects straight to you — device to device.

Five little things you can do here

🎪

The Floor

The home screen. A short list of your people, lit up green when they're around.

🪑

Rooms

Make a room, share its link, and everyone who has it lands in the same private space to chat and trade files.

📂

Flip a file

Drag a photo, a PDF, a song — anything — onto a friend's name. Sending a file is a flip; it lands straight on their disk.

🎟️

Invite a friend

Copy your room's invite link and send it however you like. They paste it in and they're with you — nothing to sign up for.

🔒

Yours, end to end

Messages and files go directly between devices, sealed. We can't read them — we can't even see they happened.

That's the core. Once you're in there's also full-text search, reactions and edits, inline file previews, delivery for messages sent while you're offline, light and dark themes, and Twin Mode to keep a second device in sync. The docs walk through all of it.

Privacy isn't a feature

Most chat apps are a company sitting between you and your friends. Fliporium isn't a company — it's just an app.

Our server only introduces peers and relays the encrypted handshake. Your messages are sealed with a key that lives in the room's invite link and never reaches us, so even the parts that can pass through us — a message left for someone offline, a relay for a tricky network — are ciphertext we can't open. No file scanning, no “ML team,” no ads.

We do count downloads and how many people are connected right now, just to see if anyone finds this useful. Those numbers are public. More in the privacy notes.

Get started in 90 seconds

  1. Download. One file: fliporium.exe. Save it anywhere — desktop, a USB stick, whatever.
  2. Run it. Double-click and you're on The Floor. Create a room, or paste an invite link to join someone else's.
  3. Invite the next person. Copy your room's invite link, send it however you like, and they're in. See how invites work.
